\begin{multicols}{2}

\textbf{Author:} BoberL

\textit{After many long-fought battles on Erathian lands, you have unfortunately been forced to flee from your kingdom. Most of your loyal guard has been captured and killed by the foes of yours. It’s only you, an artifact, that reminds you of home and one of your sergeants that have managed to escape the battlefield… or is it?}  % no-check-caps

\subsection*{\MakeUppercase{Scenario Length}}
This scenario plays out over:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{9 rounds} – on \textbf{Impossible} difficulty
  \item \textbf{8 rounds} – on \textbf{Hard} difficulty
  \item \textbf{7 rounds} – on \textbf{Normal} difficulty
  \item \textbf{7 rounds} – on \textbf{Easy} difficulty
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{\MakeUppercase{Player Setup}}
\textbf{Player Count:} 2-4

\textbf{Starting Resources:} 10 \svg{gold}, 3 \svg{building_materials}, 1 \svg{valuables}

\textbf{Starting Income:} 10 \svg{gold}, 0 \svg{building_materials}, 0 \svg{valuables}

\textbf{Starting Units:}

\begin{itemize}
  \item 1 × A ``Few'' of the most expensive \svg{bronze} Units
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Town Buildings:} There are no starting buildings in this scenario
\columnbreak
\subsection*{\MakeUppercase{Map Setup}}
Take the following Map Tiles and arrange them as shown in the Scenario map layout. \textit{$P$ stands for Player count}

\textbf{2 players:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $P$ × Starting (I) Map Tiles
  \item 2$P$ × Far (II–III) Map Tiles
  \item 2$P$ × Near (IV–V) Map Tiles
  \item 1 × Center (VI–VII) Map Tiles with Dragon Utopia
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{\MakeUppercase{Victory Conditions}}
Either:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Vanquish the enemy \textbf{Hero\textit{(es)}}
  \item Defeat the \textbf{``Dragon Utopia”} and upgrade the most expensive \svg{silver} and \svg{golden} Units.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{\MakeUppercase{Additional Rules}}
Before the start of this scenario:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Search(3)} \svg{artifact}.
\end{itemize}

During the scenario:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Gain income every round.
  \item Whenever an Obelisk is first visited, the visiting player may upgrade any \svg{silver} unit of their choice for \textbf{Free}. If this action has been already done, the player rolls \textbf{2} \svg{resource} and adds the chosen result to their Resource Pool.
\end{itemize}
